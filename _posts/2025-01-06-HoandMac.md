# Construct Summary

The authors define the uncanny valley effect as:

>"...cold, eerie feelings, associated with, but not equivalent to, fear, anxiety, and disgust, a loss of empathy, and avoidance behavior." (p. 1)

# Final Scale Items (18 total):

Items are semantic differential recorded at 7-point values ranging from -3 to +3. The eeriness factor is split into eerie and spine-tingling subfactors. Items with * are contained within the sub-factor spine-tingling.

**Perceived Humanness:** Without Definite Lifespan/Immortal, Synthetic/Real, Inanimate/Living, Mechanical Movement/Biological Movement, Human-made/Humanlike

**Attractiveness:** Messy/Sleek, Repulsive/Agreeable, Crude/Stylish, Ugly/Beautiful

**Eeriness:** Dull/Freak, Ordinary/Supernatural, Plain/Weird, Predictable/Eerie, Uninspiring/Spine-tingling*, Bland/Uncanny*, Boring/Shocking*, Predictable/Thrilling*, Unemotional/Hair-raising*
